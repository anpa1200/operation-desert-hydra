MuddyWater Cluster

Suspected state sponsor: Islamic Republic of Iran

MuddyWater is one of Iran's most active and longest-running cyber espionage clusters, linked to Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS) and active since at least 2017. US Cyber Command formally attributed MuddyWater to MOIS in February 2022. The group conducts persistent espionage operations across the Middle East, Central Asia, Europe, and beyond, with a particular focus on government, telecommunications, defense, and energy sector targets.

 

Targets and Goals

MuddyWater's targeting spans a broad geographic and sectoral range. Documented victims include government agencies, telecommunications companies, defense organizations, oil and gas firms, and managed service providers in Turkey, Saudi Arabia, Israel, UAE, Iraq, Jordan, Pakistan, Afghanistan, and multiple European and Asian countries. The group's consistent focus on government and communications infrastructure reflects MOIS's intelligence collection priorities for both foreign intelligence and domestic security purposes.

 

Tooling and Tactics

MuddyWater relies heavily on spear phishing with macro-enabled Office documents and PDF lures to deliver custom PowerShell-based implants. The group is known for using legitimate remote administration tools — including ScreenConnect, RemoteUtilities, and SimpleHelp — as part of its post-compromise toolkit, blending into normal IT traffic. Custom malware families include POWERSTATS (a PowerShell backdoor), BugSleep, and various PowerShell-based loaders. The group frequently updates its tooling to evade detection and abuses file-sharing services like OneHub and Sync for tool distribution. In 2023, a subgroup tracked as DEV-1084/Storm-1084 conducted a highly destructive operation against Israeli organizations using the DarkBit ransomware-wiper.

 

Aliases

MuddyWater is tracked across the industry as Seedworm (Symantec), TEMP.Zagros (Mandiant), Mercury and Mango Sandstorm (Microsoft), Static Kitten (Anomali), Cobalt Ulster (Secureworks), TA450 (Proofpoint), and UNC3313 (Mandiant). All refer to the same MOIS-attributed Iranian threat cluster.
